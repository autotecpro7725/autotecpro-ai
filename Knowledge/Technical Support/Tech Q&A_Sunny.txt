10) SYNC 3 Audio Issue: 

First, disconnect, unpair, and delete the original Bluetooth connection from both your phone and the vehicle’s original system.
Check that the small white audio connector behind the new unit is properly plugged in.
Confirm that the original SYNC module has been transferred correctly and installed in the proper orientation.
Make sure the original radio unit is still installed in the vehicle.
Tap Console on the new screen and verify that the original SYNC menu appears normally.
Go to Console → Radio, then play original FM or AM radio and confirm that sound is coming from all speakers.
Once the radio is playing, do not exit the radio. Simply press the Home icon in the bottom-left corner to return to the Android home screen while keeping the radio running in the background.
On the Android system, open the “HD Movies” (Video Media) app to switch the audio channel.
Finally, open CarPlay and play music to confirm that the audio from the new system is working properly.

12) SYNC Doesn't show up:

First, you will need to bring up the original SYNC menu under Console mode. There are several possible reasons why it may not be showing:

The Car Model / AC menu setting may be incorrect
The original SYNC APIM module may not have been transferred to the new system properly
The LVDS cable may not be connected properly

Can you send us a photo of the screen under Console mode? Also, does the "Original system" display properly under Console mode?
