For the Sim Card, it use the medium size sim card. In order to get a better compatibility, we would suggest you to use a full service medium size sim card. The sim card needs to have the same service as the ones we put in our phones. Also, you may need to setup an APN as well. We have tested the network on Mint Mobile / T-Mobile and they work fine. Once you insert the sim card, you can also try to use a tiny pin to reset the unit, the unit should recognize the sim automatically if it's compatible with the unit. Please also make sure the LTE antenna is located at a position where you can receive the signal as well.

Once you insert the sim card into the harness you will need to press the RST button using a thin pin tool such as a paperclip, using an improper tool can crack the screen.

If you are using the AT&T, you may need to change APN settings as well. For more details, please refer to the link below:

https://www.att.com/support/article/wireless/KM1062162

After confirming that the 4G SIM card is working properly, please make sure to set up the correct time zone as well.

1) Ensure the 4G LTE antenna is positioned where it can receive a strong signal.
2) Properly insert the SIM card into the SIM card slot.
3) Use a small pin (such as a SIM ejector tool) to press the RESET button, usually located at the front of the unit and labeled “RST.” This will restart the system.
4) Navigate to Settings > System > Android > Time Setting.
5) Enable Network Time.
6) Disable Automatic Time Zone, then manually set the time zone according to your region.
7) Verify that the time displayed in the upper-left corner of the screen matches your local time.
8) Once the time is correctly set, the internet connection should begin working.

For the time, you can also go to the WiFi section on the instruction to manually adjust the time.
